The Age of the Coven

During the Age of Vrax, a new disease spread, this one of the mind. Unlike most diseases, caused by bad scents, imbalance of the humors, or the conjunction of the heavens, Nihirizmus was an idea, a primordial truth: Life is pointless. I live and I die, and nothing I do will last eternally. And as simple as that, you are infected. This infection has no effect on it's own though. This disease comes in two parts.
No scholar knows how the first one was created, but it is abhorrent. The vampyre. Once infected with Nihirizmus, you are susceptible to contracting the same terrible nature as it's host from the bite of a vampyre. Another thing that makes this disease unique, is that it is the only thing the wonder of incarnation cannot heal. Once a body is a vampyre, a body is ever a vampyre.
As vampyrism spread in the Age of Vrax, a new group formed, the Coven, a coalition of vampyres in Douris. As it grew, it set it sights on the isle of Myst. Vampyres burn in the sunlight, and a place where the sun never rises was viewed as a safe haven for their kind. And so the Coven infiltrated Myst, and planned an assault, to be led by their Arch-Vampyre, the most powerful vampyre to walk Ikkown, a sorcerer and swordsman. For 6 barters war raged in Myst, the Drow who had made it their home and the vampyres seeking theirs.
The vampyres won. All had gone in their favor. But in a desperate moment, the governor of Myst sacrificed what the Drow valued most to save their land: independence. He called upon Raxake to aid their fight. Within hours, the full legions of Raxake arrived on Myst's shores, outnumbering the Coven 12 to 1. It was a bloodbath. When the Coven was defeated, the Arch-Vampyre was brought to Raxake, tied to a stake upon the highest mountain to watch the sunrise, and burn to only ashes. The ashes were placed in the vampyre's home crypt in a small fishing town in Myst, if only to a recognize a true warrior, even if an abhorrent beast.
This was the first war.
Once the threat had passed, Raxake's military did not return to Douris. The weakened Drow could not fight them back, and so Myst became a vassal state of Raxake.

Outline real

10 chapters (nothing to do with 10 deliverable assignments, chosen well before that, it's because 10 is max level. leveling up at the end of each chapter). Each chapter has a Teelul name with it's common translation. It goes unstated that throughout the main plot, I also will add some side quests and companion quests; this is a primary campaign plot summary.

  • Penreim (Pilgrim)
    • The Pilgrim (how the book will refer to the pc, newly incarnated people in Ikkown are named after where they incarnated until they come into their own and name themselves) incarnates in Penreim crypt in Mebut, a sleepy little fishing town on the northern tip of Myst.
    • The Pilgrim meets Veritas in said crypt and she is the first companion.
    • They leave the crypt and stay in Mebut for a day, before heading to the city of Capidum to register the Pilgrim.
      • Some side quests and foreshadowing are in Mebut
    • When staying at the inn that night, you are overcome by your vampyrism and must feed, discovering your affliction. who you feed on has consequences as Veritas has Nihirizmus and will become a vampyre if you feed on her.
  • Dokutum (Purpose)
    • The Pilgrim travels to Capidum to register, but the city is in the midst of revolt. Revolutionary drow seek Myst's independence from Raxake. The gates are closed the party must pass through the Undercity to get to Capidum
    • 2 parts
      • The Wilds
        • In the wild on their way to the undercity the party encounters William and Geoffrey (rounding out a full 4 man party before reaching the undercity)
      • The Undercity (see map at the bottom)
        • The Undercity is a slum inside a giant cave beneath Capidum, and the original home of the Drow who were banished from Douris
        • In the Undercity, the party will be introduced personally to the 3 major factions
          • Revolutionaries: Drow who want Myst back
          • Loyalist: Raxaken nobles quelling rebellion
          • The Coven: existence is known to the pc at this point, not much else. They request meeting with the pc in Capidum once the pc enters the Undercity
        • The pcs motivation changes here. Obviously registration is out of the question during an uprising, but as the pc is a vampyre, they are involved with Coven activities by association whether they like it or not
        • Getting into Capidum requires help from one of the 3 factions
          • Haven't really dug that deep yet, idk what those 3 paths are
          • Somewhere in here you also meet more companions
        • Chapter ends upon entering Capidum
  • Peccai Orfale (Original Sin)
    • This chapter revolves around resolving the revolution.
    • The characters may (and are encouraged too) meet with the Coven in Capidum. Here they will learn the Coven is back stronger and once again wishes to take Myst for their own. They want to recruit the pc bc they are the Arch-Vampyre, but they do not tell the pc this yet
    • 3 paths to resolving the revolution:
      • Loyalists
        1. pc helps assassinate the revolutionary leader
        2. Coven is pulling the loyalists strings and the Coven takes over Myst
      • Revolutionaries
        1. pc helps assassinate the Raxaken governor
        2. Coven swoops in in the disarray with overwhelming force and takes control of Myst
      • Coven
        1. pc helps assassinate the Raxaken governor explicitly for the Coven to take over Myst
        2. Coven takes over Myst
    • No matter what the Coven will take Myst by storm
    • Depending on which faction you side with, they will offer you a companion to assist who will stay with you afterwards
      • By the end of this chapter you have had a chance to recruit all the companions
  • Nihirizmus (Nihilism) & Suki bum Suki (I am that I Am)
    • Non-plot notes
      • These 2 chapters blend together because there aren't any distinct fights yet. I'm not sure where I will draw the line.
      • This is where theology comes into play.
      • This is also where the story really starts to branch and so from here onward my descriptions will be more and more vague. I plan to makes flow charts for each chapter and the plot as a whole eventually.
      • At this point, your companions do not know you are a vampyre, but your companions and people you meet have made their minds very clear about vampyres with all this talk of the Coven.
        • Nihilism has been drilled home. You (the vampyre, the sinner) are a worthless wretch
      • Each companions individual story also has a midpoint at which they are infected with Nihirizmus: they realize the Numein are false, and their life is pointless. That midpoint for most of them is somewhere around here.
    • At the start of this chapter, your Arch-Vampyrism is revealed.
      • Depending on your previous allegiances
        • The Coven will tell you
        • The Vobidashi, a vampyre splinter group who seek something greater in life will
    • This is all tactless and too your companions faces. They will react accordingly (they will not turn to violence, as they all have Nihirizmus and are questioning their worldview)
    • Through the Vobidashi, you will also learn of Helen Cagth Suporits. She is the new Arch-Vampyre of the Coven.
      • Helen is a theological rock in this story (not in a good way). She long ago
        1. Contracted Nihirizmus and vampyrism
        2. Concluded the Numein are false deities
        3. Concluded there must be a greater being
        4. Concluded "kiri bum suki" (loosely translates to "fuck God")
        5. Decided to take "kiri bum suki" literally and attempt to kill God
        • By "theological rock", I mean thus: Helen is a Nihirizmus infected power hungry vampyre who knows her life is worthless. She is the pc. The problem is that by the end of the story, she will not be able to overcome her pride and bow down before a great God.
    • Unknown to most of the Coven, Helen aims to drink the blood of the Numein and call The Forgotten One down and kill him.
      • Numein note:
        • The Numein are demigods who are literally in Ikkown. You can roll up to Libeva's temple in Lethuis and she is literally there. I do this because I think it helps personify what each of them represent very strongly.
      • If you sided with the Coven previously, she will tell you herself.
      • The Vobidashi will inform you most likely otherwise.
    • Since the creation of Ikkown, The Forgotten One has fallen silent for generations. This is his first appearance in recorded history.
      • The Forgotten One will appear to the pc at this central point. This is when everything will be made clear. The Numein are false, The Forgotten One created all, and still rules almighty. Helen can suck all the Numein blood she wants, she can't kill God but she's still gonna be really powerful and it will be pretty crappy for everyone else.
        • Fun gameplay tie-in: The Forgotten One is a Warlock patron that becomes accessible at this point
    • At the end of this section, whether you side with The Forgotten One or not, Helen must be stopped from consuming the Numein's blood cause that's basically the end of the world as we know it.
  • 6-8: Valsai Kwaarum, Valsai Yonum, & Valsai Mathia (False Evil, False Good, & False Apathy)
    • These chapters can be played in any order at this point
      • These chapters are effectively 7 boss fights against the parts of the Coven Helen has sent to retrieve Numein blood. Which order the Coven goes to them is chosen at random, but Madiven is always last. The party can also choose the order they wish to visit the Numein. Each Numein has one of 3 outcomes
        1. The Coven got there first, and it's too late
        2. Party and Coven arrive at the same time, combat ensues.
        3. Party arrives first and warns the Numein of what will happen.
          • The party may stay with a Numein to defend them personally, just warning a Numein does not guarantee the Coven won't get to them
    • Yonum and Kwaarum are the Triad and Triptych respectively, each centered in one of the major citites, each city having 3 major temples, 1 for each Numein. This effectively boils down to 6 timed side quests in 2 different cities and 1 major quest on Madiven's throne island
    • How many Numein the party saves will determine the difficulty of Helen's final fight. If they fail to save any of them, it's practically impossible. If they save 7, Helen will be a pushover (statistically nigh impossible). The party is expected to save ~3
  • Shi no Umbruw no Moorti (The Valley of the Shadow of the Death)
    • The Valley of the Shadow of the Death is where the thinnest point in the veil between Okultos and Ikkown rest, and where Helen will go after the Numein are dealt with. The players will be level 10 here. This chapter is for the most part a dungeon crawl to Helen's boss fight.
