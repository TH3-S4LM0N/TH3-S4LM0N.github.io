As the body burns, a zimashi exits—a life force, soul as some call it. But souls are not of the physical realm, and the tie between the physical and the spiritual is snipped.
The spiritual is not something easily considered by the mind of man. Okultos is what their limited tongues call it, but a name cannot be applied to existence itself. As the soul dives deep into an abyss of spacelessness, time passes, stands till, and reverses. For the spirit is eternal. No, perpetual. Eternity spans in both directions, but existence is still an immutable point. Okultos is not trifled by this concept of the present. It is perpetual.
Within the spiritual lie many entities. Zimashi. Souls yet to be made manifest and souls whose body is past them exist simultaneously. Yet more souls exist. Souls which will never be made physical. They hold the true power, as they want not for the flesh. Above them all, an Emperor sits upon his throne. Amidst them all, an evil stirs chaos. Below them all, something greater waits. Dormant, but not imperceptive. It has laid silent for millions of souls, but it's rest is short to come.
But in Ikkown, much time has passed, and body has healed. Incarnation begins. As a new soul is called from Okultos, and begins to breathe life into the once-burnt body, the rest of mankind wallows, just as it has for all time before.
The Numein sit on their thrones. Valsai Kwaarum. The Triptych. Beauty and indulgence incarnate. A true worshipping of the self. Valsai Yonum. The Triad. Good and love manifest. Putting the greater good of others before ones self. A wonderful pastime. Valsai Mathia. In this rests the true power. Madiven sits above all, unquestionably the greatest of the Numein; yet he is neutral. He has no greater purpose. He is a fraud, just as they all are. Powerless over death or time. False evil, false good, false apathy.
In Teirium, one man gathers a multitude. Driven by hunger, they hover as an impending doom over free civilization. In Douris, they are ignorant. They refuse to believe that there could be war. The very people whose ancestors so long ago left their neighbors land under their conviction of meifesta sorei are now threatened by one with an even stronger belief: they must fill their stomachs. The wanton study of sorcery and the spiritual practiced in Douris will not fill stomachs, nor will it push back a hundred swords. But for now, the Teirians are plump, and have time to prepare.
And in a moment, life is brought a sliver closer to it's inevitable end; one vampyre becomes two. The wonder of incarnation can heal most any wound, and rebuild a body from the ash of a stake-burned traitor. But it cannot cure vampyrism. It is simply that body's nature, no matter the soul. And so Nihirizmus takes another. When there is no blood left to feed? Maybe new life will take hold. Maybe a body will be incarnated, simply to thirst for something which does not exist and die. A cruel world it is.
But towards the isle of Myst, a strange darkness lurks. Impenetrable by the spiritual and physical eye. For once the sun never rose on Douris or it's sister isle. But light shines on the land now. The sun never rose over Myst though, and so darkness gathers there. An assured evil lives there. Deep in the crypts of Myst, a possibly greater evil lies dormant. It's awakening will spell catastrophe.
But this soul is not going to any of the those places. Instead, it's body slumbers in the deep. In the ancient city of Telok, below the surface of the waves. The Telsh have sparked the first war with Teirium, and Telok is not long for this world. Teirium's sorcery will shake the earth and bring the ground in upon itself, and the Telsh will flee to land. But the Telsh are an inconsequential race in the world of Ikkown.
And so it is. Physical and spiritual. Ut bona ut instaata.
