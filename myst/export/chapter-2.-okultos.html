Chapter 2. Okultos

WH40K Warp Inspiration

Okultos being the source of 'magic' is (heavily) inspired by the Warp/Immaeterium from Warhammer 40K. There are a few common lore explanations for magic in fantasy settings:

  • Manifestation of willpower (fine but bland)
  • It just exists (I like "this isn't irl earth, things are different because they are" explanations)
  • Some sort of divine source
  • Manipulation of cosmic energy

In Warhammer 40K, while it's not called magic, magic exists through psychic abilities (which is reffered to as sorcery often). In Warhammer, the Immaeterium is an parrallel dimension of pure psychic and emotional energy, and harnessing that energy is the key to being a psyker. Okultos will act similar.
I like this much more than magic because it feels much more gritty and grounded. Psychic energy and such (while not real) has a basis in real life, with peoples belief in souls and the like. It feels like something that could evolve and isn't as fantastical as a magic just exists explanation.

Only one written record exists trying to explain Okultos. In ———, a wizened sorcerer, soon to reach his death, choose to finish his life's work by gaining true knowledge of Okultos. He bonded his and his apprentice's eyes, and focus a dominion granting him one wish. As expected, the sorcerer died, but his apprentice viewed the events through his eyes, and wrote the following after the experience:
"It was a gaping maw, being infinite in all directions, but existing only for a fraction of a moment—a single point in time, immeasurable, but containing everything."
The apprentice never practiced sorcery again. His fate is unknown.

- A History of Sorcery, Voxriss

Okultos is a world parallel to mans understanding and the laws of Ikkown; it is formed of pure energy, wrought by the suffering of the living in Ikkown—Okultos is made of pure emotion and psyche. The existence of Okultos is a fact known among all of the inhabitants of Ikkown, and while known by all, it is understood by a very few, and no man has every grasped it's true nature.
Okultos does not follow any laws of Ikkown. Man interprets Ikkown as a space with 4 dimensions—3 spatial which they can move through, and time, which they cannot—but Okultos is not so. Okultos is 5 dimensional, and the beings within it can move through 4. While a physical humanoid simply cannot exist in Okultos, the 4th dimension, time, connects both worlds. But, since Okultos' creatures can move through this 4th dimension, humanoids perceive Okultos as a place where time is fluid, and does not flow consistently with Ikkown, or at a consistent rate at all.
While Okultos is not inherently something to be feared, it is not something to turn your back on either. Okultos is filled with entities that only exist through mans emotions, and as such man must keep on feeling for them to exist; and pain is easier to bring than pleasure. The energies of Okultos are not all to mans detriment though. Some have learned to harness their psychic power and connection to Okultos, utilizing it in a form known as sorcery.

The Destruction Paradox

Okultos thrives on the triumphs and failures of mankind, and it rests on a fine balance. Most of the entities of Okultos seek to bring suffering upon Ikkown, simply because it is easier than seeking joy. This poses a problem: If Okultos were to bring the ultimate suffering, the death of mankind, it would be Okultos' ultimate victory. But it would bring their downfall in the same moment, as the end of mankind means the end of the psychic energy Okultos needs to exist. Okultos is divided on whether it is a worthwhile price to pay.

Sorcery

Sorcery

Terminology

  • Exerts an aptitude: sounds dumb
  • Cast an aptitude: Too magic-coded
  • Actualizes an aptitude: Big word
  • Enforce an aptitude: gets across the reality bending nature, but a little weird

Sorcery is the greatest boon Okultos has brought to mankind. Harnessing the psychic ability of man is a challenge, as not all have enough power to practice sorcery. It takes training from a young age to master it, but it is a powerful tool.
Different moral views of sorcery crop up from those who do not practice it. Some are apathetic, a tool is a tool, no matter it's form. Others see utilizing sorcery as colluding with the entities of Okultos, and abetting the downfall of mankind. Most governments do not take such a stance, as even with the dangers it poses, sorcery is too great a gift too overlook. Most governments require sorcerers to be sanctioned and carry a warrant, allowing them to practice.

Annals of Sorcery

Sorcery is a well studied phenomena, and scholars have grouped and categorized all forms of it by three different rankings of power—how difficult it is too perform and how much effect it has on the world.
Cantrips are minor focus of psychic forces, that can be performed by anyone with some potential and a little training. Use of a cantrip generally does not make one a sorcerer.
Aptitudes are the majority of sorcery. Aptitudes are scaled from one through five, and concern all manner of psychic phenomena and ability.
Dominions are the greatest feat a sorcerer can perform. A dominion wears thin the boundary between Ikkown and Okultos, the alter the very fabric of the world as man understands it. Successful usage of a dominion often kills the sorcerer. Even if they survive, they will never practice sorcery again. Along with their power, all sorcery is also grouped by its annal, or the domain it affects. Sorcerers often specialize in an annal, devoting their life to mastering it's secrets.
Purity manipulates ones own psyche and enforces it upon Ikkown. While pure magic is the most powerful, it is also constrained by the limits of possibility. Things that are impossible are impossible without letting Okultos in.
Reality lets Okultos in. This sorcery utilizes the unnatural existence of Okultos to do things that should be seemingly impossible by mans knowledge of Ikkown. Reality takes Okultos' energy and creates something from nothing.
Life focuses on the contact of one entities psyche with another. It can heal or hurt, bring back the dead and influence the living. Life though, has no effect on anything that does not have it's own psychic connection to Okultos.

Occuli

While most sorcerers rely on innate talent, some with less energy, but the capability of knowledge utilize occuli—petrified brains of Okultos creatures. Harnessing the nature of a being of pure psychic energy made manifest, they imprint their aptitudes upon it's still living flesh, bending it to their will. Occuli require immense training to use, and a sorcerer who has mastered them is known as an Occulus.

Psionics

While the psychic energy of sorcery is closely tied to the sorcerer, it can be imbued into an item, losing much of it's power. These objects are known as psionics. While the energy is separated from it's sorcerer, it retains a thin link. Because of such, when a humanoid comes in direct contact with a psionic, they feel the emotions of it's creator, far in the back of their mind. This phenomena has been studied in an attempt to read minds or communicate, but nothing has come of it.

Illusions

Some aptitudes create illusions of varying quality and perceivability. Mastery of illusions is measured in 2 parts: minor or major, and ethereal or perfect.
Minor illusions are of little stature, no larger than a 4 square feet. Most minor illusions are of inanimate objects such as secret walls or doors, but can also be of small living things. Minor illusions often do not require their exerters full concentration.
Major illusions are any illusion larger than a minor one, and require much more sorcerous ability to maintain. They can range from a humanoid to a whole building (GM discretion), as long as the sorcerer can concentrate on it.
Ethereal illusions exist visually and auditorily. Ethereal illusions are detected immediately when coming close enough to touch them, or with a DC passive Perception check.
Perfect illusions are effectively summoning, but for that they only last as long as the sorcerer concentrates. For all intents and purposes, they are real. Ethereal illusions can only be detected if one has reason to believe there is an illusion at play, and even then, they are still just as real.

Incarnation

Numein do not cause incarnation

They claim they do and people believe it, but The Forgotten One created Okultos and the process of incarnation after the Numein messed up

While Okultos is, to an extent, the seat of man's fears, it is also the source of man's life. Okultos plays an irreplicable role in the beautiful process known as incarnation. When the Numein created Ikkown, they made 77,777 bodies, and placed souls within them. But eventually those bodies withered and died, and their souls formed Okultos. But the emotions of the first lives ever lived created more souls. The Numein saw this and thought it was very pure, and so they started the cycle of incarnation.
When a body dies in Ikkown, it's soul moves on to Okultos. The body is then placed in a crypt, and over around twenty barters, the body will heal. Even from a death so destructive as burning to ash, the form can be regrown from a shard of a fingernail, though it may take upwards of hundreds of barters. Once the body is prepared to live again, a newly made soul will be cast into it from Okultos, and the body will live again.
Incarnation is an invasive process. The new soul does not share any memory of the form's previous host, but some skill is retained. Knowledge mastered to the point of muscle memory may be carried over to a new life, manifesting in the form of a natural giftedness towards those subjects. Common knowledge is also retained, similar to effects like amnesia. A newly incarnated person knows of the Numein and of Myst and Teirium, but they do not understand, as they have no experience.
Names in Ikkown are chosen by the person when they feel they have completed a task in life that defines them as a person. Till then, the new incarnate often adopts the name of the crypt they incarnated in. Names are powerful, and choosing one should not be taken lightly. Once an action that truly identifies a person is completed and they choose their name, it is quite literally set in stone on the walls of the Annals of Voxriss.

Entities

Life—if it can be called that—abounds in Okultos just as it does in Ikkown. Although it takes a different form, similar principles exist in the society of Okultos. While a culture as complex as Ikkown exists, only three entities have the power to make contact with Ikkown and be known.

The Emperor

The Emperor, whose Teelul name is Keizor'no, is known to be the greatest power in Okultos. He rules with an iron fist, ensuring Ikkown produces enough energy for Okultos to thrive, but preventing the Destruction Paradox. He often pacts with warlocks of Ikkown, and at times has even been active in the politics of Ikkown through his warlocks.

The Demoness

The Demoness is the, to use Ikkown's way or perceiving, 'young' rebel looking to overthrow the Emperor. She is rabid for bloodshed in Ikkown, throwing caution to the wind. She does not believe in the Destruction Paradox, preaching that the ultimate victory would end Okultos' reliance on man to survive.

The Forgotten One

The Forgotten One refers to an entity that at one point existed and interacted with Ikkown, but has not been recorded on Ikkown since before the Meifesta Sorei. Recorded is a strong word here. Myths and rumors speak of his existence, but it is generally regarded as heresy among followers of the Numein, and the credibility of those who speak of these accounts is hogwash at best because they weren't alive thousands of barters ago.

Talking about The Forgotten One

The Forgotten One's existence is rumored, but by the 3rd barter of the Age of the Downfall of Telok, he is nearly entirely lost to history. Man has not even considered that something of his nature could exist, as it is a well guarded secret by the Numein.
All references to The Forgotten One before chapter 5 of the campaign should be vague at best. The player must believe they are alone in the world.
