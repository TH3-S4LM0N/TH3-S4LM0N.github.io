Chapter 1. Penreim

Pilgrim

Penreim (pen-rame) is a crypt near the quiet fishing outpost of Mebut, on the northern tip of Myst. The newly incarnate leaving the crypt is a rare but not surprising sight, and the village is a welcoming place for those new to this world, providing a place to stay while they gather their bearings. And it is so for the Pilgrim; for as is customary in Ikkown, the character will be referred by the common translation of their crypt's name: Pilgrim.
Chapter 1 covers the incarnation and beginning of life for the Pilgrim, and their stay in Mebut before venturing into the wider world to seek purpose in life. In it they will meet companions to bring, and discover a bastard curse at their very core.

Penreim

This tale begins with incarnation. Read the following:

In the cold and damp dark of a tarnished casket, you arise. The wood is not nailed down, and slides easily, allowing you to stand straight. You are alive.
You stand within a crypt, walls inlaid with many a casket same as your own. In the shadows there is complete silence. If you close your eyes you could believe you were not alive, but the stale, dead air against your skin says otherwise.

Penreim is split into three rooms; two crypts, and one entrance hall with a shrine to the Numein. In the deepest room of Penreim, the Pilgrim incarnates.
Equipment. Next to their casket lays gifts and relics of their previous inhabitant, including various equipment pieces. This includes the starting equipment from the characters creation.
Veritas. As the Pilgrim enters the second room, they may see a figure on the other side of the room with a Perception check of 15, lowering as they get closer to the figure. Once the Pilgrim makes themself known, Veritas will spin with dagger in hand, threatening not to get any closer, thinking the Pilgrim is a guardsmen or another grave robber like herself. She will relax upon hearing the Pilgrim is neither thing. As is customary, she will offer the Pilgrim a helping hand in getting their footing in this new life if the Pilgrim doesn't turn her over for grave robbery. Upon this agreement, she will become the first companion to join the Pilgrim's journey.
Wendigo. As the party approaches the shrine room of the crypt, a Perception check of 14 will reveal a scuffling sound, like a large creature ripping into something. Inside the room is a Wendigo, ripping into a fresh corpse laid in the crypt. It is weakened, with only half it's normal Hit Points, and obvious crossbow wounds. In it's injured form it's Strength is reduced to 8, and it's bite attack only does 1c4-1 damage. Upon the party entering the room, it will immediately charge the Pilgrim and attempt to bite it. After a taste of the Pilgrim's flesh though, the Wendigo will turn and flee in disgust. Unbeknownst to the party at the time, the Pilgrim's nature as a vampyre makes an atrocious meal for a Wendigo.
Exit. After the Wendigo flees, the party will exit into the permanent night air of Myst and see the lights of Mebut down towards the shoreline. Upon exiting Penreim, the party levels up.

Mebut

Mebut is a sleepy fishing outpost on the northern tip of Myst, it's only draw being that it is the closest place one can dock when sailing from Lethuis. It's residents are well accustomed to the newly incarnate, living so near to a crypt, although it is still not a common occurrence.
Veritas. Upon entering Mebut, Veritas will inform the Pilgrim that she has a room in the Sleeping Giant inn, and she is headed there to rest, and the Pilgrim may stay with her for a rest. Afterwards they must head to Capidum, the governing city of Myst to register the Pilgrim.
Raxaken Military. A ship of Raxaken soldiers returning from their drafted service has docked in Mebut just hours ago, and most of them are milling around Mebut, in the Sleeping Giant, or still by the ship.

A. Sleeping Giant Inn

Sleeping Giant

It's Sleeping Giant but you could also take it as a Skyrim reference.

The Sleeping Giant inn is, like the rest of Mebut, not soon to wake up. The tavern is rather lively this travel though, as the returned soldiers make merry with the meager stock of drink the Giant has to offer.
Soldiers. The soldiers filling the Sleeping Giant are mildly imbibed and open to conversation. Upon discovering the Pilgrim is newly incarnate, they drunkenly sing a strange song about life and death that is culturally sung in celebration of the incarnate. The soldiers then begin to talk about Raxaken politics, home, and the terrors of existence, and the Pilgrim may listen in. Their questions about the new world will be gladly answered by the soldiers.
Geoffrey. In a corner of Sleeping Giant away from the soldiers rabble is a man polishing a fine crossbow with a grim look on his face. Upon approach, he will snap out of it and greet the Pilgrim warmly. He recognizes the Pilgrim is not a soldier, and not one of the regular faces of the Giant, and asks what brings them to Mebut. Upon learning that the Pilgrim is headed to Capidum, he proposes they travel together, as he is headed in that direction as well. If the Pilgrim accepts, he will join the party's travels.
Gudus. At one of the tables, a game of low-stakes Gudus is taking place. Here the Pilgrim will be taught by one of the soldiers and may have their first game.
William. If the Pilgrim did not encounter William at the Docks, they will find him in the Sleeping Giant. See B. Docks for details.

Resting

When the Pilgrim is ready to lay their head, they may rest in Veritas' rented room.
In the midst of their rest, the Pilgrim will awake. Read the following.

You awake with a burning desire, a hunger. Not a need for meat or mead though, but a feeling as though the very essence of your existence is seeping through the cracks in your ribcage.
Blood. All at once, instinct takes over. You need blood.

Unbeknownst to the Pilgrim, their vampyrism has awakened after it's long sleep in the crypt. They must feed. The Pilgrim may make a Lore check; a 10 will recognize their condition as vampyrism, while a 15 will bring to mind important knowledge of Nihirizmus and how the spread of their curse may happen.
Veritas. Veritas is the easiest target, in the same room as the Pilgrim. Veritas does believe Nihirizmus though, and feeding on her will turn her, but the Pilgrim does not necessarily know this, though they may have inferred it through a combination of Lore and Insight, to know of Nihirizmus and recognize it in Veritas respectively.
Soldiers. All throughout the Sleeping Giant, soldiers are resting or drinking or gambling. An isolated one of them would make easy pickings.
Mebut. Outside the Giant, while most of the town is asleep, some sailors or merchants are still at work, while various degenerates roam the streets.
Abstinence. A Pilgrim who passes a Wisdom check of DC 18 may exert their willpower and hold themselves from sating their thirst. They will suffer from the effects of abstinence though (==placeholder for a backlink to the vampyre doc on chastity).

After slaking their palette, the Pilgrim returns to a not-quite-peaceful rest. Upon resting, the party levels up, and chapter 2 ends.

B. Docks

At the docks, a ship of discharged Raxaken soldiers has docked, and is currently unloading various belongings and other goods sent with it for transport. The captain of this regiment (Veteran) is overseeing the proceedings.
William. Standing near the ship is a rare sight. A Telsh soldier who seems rather lost is searching his pack. On approach he kindly and sternly greets the Pilgrim, asking if they know the way to Capidum. William is looking for a traveling partner to return to Capidum and see his family along the way. If the Pilgrim accepts, William will join the party and stay the night at the Sleeping Giant.
