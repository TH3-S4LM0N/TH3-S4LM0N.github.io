Numein Representation

The Numein are gods but not God. The Numein represent the 3 major categories of wordly things people worship instead of the real God. The Numein only have the power people give them, they have effect on Ikkown, but extend no more.

The Numein are the major super-humanoid forces at work in Ikkown, and they are worshiped as gods; they are not.
Many humanoids worship and revere the Numein, as their power over humanoids and Ikkown seems god-like, and to an extent, it is. They have great control over the lives of man and the happenings in Ikkown, but their power is very strongly limited. The average mind of man fails to see the bigger picture; the Numein may control worldly events, but that is all. At most, the Numein bring fleeting happiness, but they have not defeated death. Followers of the Numein ultimately prostrate themselves before beings whose power is just as limited as their own, but have deceived the fool into believing it can make something of nothing.
The Numein are sevenfold, separated into three groups, Madiven, the Triad, & the Triptych. Madiven is the foremost of them all, but is also the most neutral. While Madiven is worshiped, his worshipers do not believe in much besides him, and he is a largely neutral if not apathetic entity. The Triad is made of Vritsu, Dikum, and Mortud, and represents a largely altruistic view of existence. The Triptych exists on the opposite side, where Libeva, Geluna, and Shizuur offer a self-gratifying worldview.

A Myth

Once, a regionally popular bard in Douris sang a ballad, claiming to be the story of how the Numein and Ikkown came to be. It was played once, met with cries of "dunt lunus" and "meugloval". And the bard was mocked out of his career. While the ballad's story was met with jeers by the commoners and priests of the Numein, the few who understood the true state of existence knew of it's truth. The following is a summary of it's contents, as the original verse has been lost to time. The ballad was entitled Madiven, the Triad, & the Triptych, or the Vices of Man. \

O one we have forgot!
Remind us of a tale,
Preerus! Who suffered not
the lies of our fallen gods.

And so he stood, before
Madiven, one they call
Creator! Lord! And god!
One of three, no falsehood.

"Give up this task in vain,
your woeful existence
means not but to be slain,
betrayed by one you call
Schietor"

Two cycles of the heavens before,
Ikkown was naught but a thought.
Only had there been, Madiven,[1]
The Triad, The Triptych.

Half-gods of empty worlds,
created by one truth,
by power eternal
the one called Schietor.

But these seven Numein,
they could not bow down.
Schietor offered them
freedom, but one command:

"Do not create life, or you shall be cast down among it."

But the Numein in pride,
brought upon themselves their fall.
When Preerus, the first man,
was made, Schietor called.

"Preerus.
Come and see the downfall of your creators."

His voice booms like thunder,
and Preerus can do naught,
but fall prostrate in awe
and wonder before glory.

On his journey to the
place Schietor called him,
Preerus met temptation.
Both good and evil.
